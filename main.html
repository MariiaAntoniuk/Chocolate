Chocolate

The basis of chocolate is cocoa - a type of evergreen tree from the Theobroma genus.
From Latin, the word "theobroma" is translated as "food of the gods" (theos - god, broma - food).
Chocolate trees are very heat-loving because
they only grow near the equator in humid tropical forests where there is a lot of sun and rainfall.
Flowers appear directly on the trunk of the tree, and after pollination by insects - fruits.
As soon as the fruits are ripe, they are cut,
opened and the white slimy seeds are removed, which will later become cocoa beans.
Thousands of years ago, beans were dried in the sun, where they acquired a brownish-red color.
Oddly enough, today this stage takes place in the same way.
After drying, the cocoa beans were ground and added to water.
It was the first version as a bitter fortifying drink with foam.
It was not very similar to what we are used to drinking today from the look of yakoo.
In the Middle Ages in Europe, the powder was considered a cure for indigestion, coughs and colds.
Then sugar cane or honey, spices (chili pepper, cinnamon, etc.)
were added to it, ahead of the trend for today's popular spicy cocoa in fashionable coffee shops.
